Fullplay Media Library Help

The Fullplay Media Library (FML) is an application that enables media content stored on your PC to be played on your Fullplay Digital Jukebox (DJ) over your home network.  In addition, the FML application provides a portal for you to choose internet radio stations to listen to on your DJ.  Please take a moment to read over this documentation to familiarize yourself with the operation of FML.

Finding content on your PC

Use the music finder window to select the drives and directories that contain your content.  The quickest way to find all your content is to select all your hard drives.  All the subfolders from the ones you pick will be searched for content too.

When you have selected the drives and directories that contain your content, click the scan for music button.  A window will pop up and show the content items as they are found.

Naming your media library

You can run multiple FML applications on multiple PCs.  The name that you give your media library identifies this particular FML to your DJ.  You can change the default name provided to one of your liking.  Just click on the edit box for the media library name to begin editing. 

If you would like your DJ to be aware of your new media library name right away, click the Save Changes button.  If you don’t click on the Save Change button right away, the changes will be automatically updated after FML is idle for a little while.

You are now ready to play the content on your PC from your DJ.  Before you do this though, you can create playlists of your favorite content on your PC and also select internet radio stations.

Creating playlists

Click on the my music files button to explore the content that FML found on your PC and create playlists of your favorites. 

Using the music available window, you can browse the content that FML found on your PC and create playlists.  Go ahead and try browsing your content.  When you are ready to create a playlist, there are a couple of ways you can do this.

Select an item in the music available window by clicking on it with your mouse.  You can select any folder or individual item.  Drag the item into the playlists window.  You can now go ahead and edit the name of the playlist, although you can always change the name later.  If you select a folder, all individual items within that folder are added to the playlist that is created. To add content to a playlist, just drag a folder or item onto an existing playlist.  The playlist you are adding to will be highlighted. 

You can also create an empty playlist by clicking on the Add List button.  You can then drag folders and items into this playlist. 

To change the name of an existing playlist, select the playlist and then either click on the Edit List button, or press F2 to begin editing the name.  All names must be unique and the editor will help you do this. 

If you would like your DJ to be aware of your new playlists right away, click the Save Changes button.  If you don’t click on the Save Change button right away, the changes will be automatically updated after FML is idle for a little while.

Tip: If you have a lot of content you can find particular artists, albums or genres by expanding the part of the music available window that you are interested in and typing the first few letters of what you are looking for. Also, if you double-click on any individual track, it will start playing in your default player for that particular file type.

Choose Internet radio stations

Click on the internet radio button to choose internet radio stations.  The radio stations available window shows the directory of radio stations that are currently available. 

Note: Your firewall settings may prevent FML from retrieving this list of radio stations from the Fullplay Media server.  Please refer to the section on setting up your firewall for details.  If an error occurs, a message will be displayed prompting you to press F5 to retry.  Adjust your firewall settings, then press F5 to retry getting the radio station list from the Fullplay Media server. 

Drag and drop the radio stations that interest you to the my radio stations window.  The stations in the my radio stations window are the ones that your DJ will see. 

If you know of other radio stations that are not shown in the radio stations available window, you can add them to your list of radio stations by clicking on the Add Station button.  You can then enter the name and URL for the station.

You can change the name or URL of a radio station in the my radio stations window by selecting it, and then either pressing F2 or clicking on the Edit Station button.

If you would like your DJ to be aware of your new radio stations right away, click the Save Changes button.  If you don’t click on the Save Change button right away, the changes will be automatically updated after FML is idle for a little while.  If you double-click any radio stations, it will play in your default internet radio player.

Firewall settings

A firewall is a program that controls how applications can access, and be accessed from the Internet and your local network.  If you use a firewall, you will need to enable FML to access the Internet and act as a server on your local network.  FML uses ports 1900 (UDP), 2900 (TCP), and 3030 (TCP).  Please refer to your firewall’s documentation for enabling FML to utilize these ports.
